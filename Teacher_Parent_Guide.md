### WEEK 1: DINOSAUR IDENTIFICATION (60 minutes)

#### SETUP (Before Session)
- [ ] Print 15 dinosaur cards (including silhouettes)
- [ ] Laminate for reuse
- [ ] Create 6 floor/table sorting stations
- [ ] Print 10-question quiz
- [ ] Have timer ready

#### SESSION TIMELINE

**Minutes 0-5: Arrival & Energy Builder**
- Play upbeat music as kids arrive
- Show amazing dinosaur image
- Ask: "What makes each dinosaur special?"

**Minutes 5-10: Intro Activity**
- Show all 15 dino silhouettes quickly
- Kids shout out names they know
- "Today you'll become dinosaur experts!"

**Minutes 10-25: Teaching (Teach 3 concepts)**

1. **Diet Identification** (5 min)
   - Show T. rex teeth (sharp) vs Triceratops teeth (flat)
   - Carnivore = meat-eater = sharp teeth
   - Herbivore = plant-eater = flat teeth
   - Have kids pretend to eat (carnivore growls, herbivore munches)

2. **Era Timeline** (5 min)
   - Draw simple timeline: TRIASSIC â†’ JURASSIC â†’ CRETACEOUS
   - "Oldest to newest = bottom to top in rock"
   - Draw simple climate: hot/dry â†’ tropical â†’ hot/humid

3. **Key Traits** (5 min)
   - Show images of horns, plates, armor
   - Connect to dinosaurs: 3 horns = Triceratops, plates = Stegosaurus
   - Traits = adaptations = how they survived

**Minutes 25-50: Main Activity - DINO SORTING RELAY**

Setup (5 min):
- Create 6 stations with cards:
  * Triassic Carnivores
  * Triassic Herbivores
  * Jurassic Carnivores
  * Jurassic Herbivores
  * Cretaceous Carnivores
  * Cretaceous Herbivores

Activity (20 min):
- Divide into 2-3 teams
- Each student grabs one dinosaur card
- Runs to correct bin based on diet + era
- Returns to get next card
- Round takes 5 minutes
- Do 2-3 rounds, fastest + most accurate wins

Debrief (2 min):
- "Tell me which station is for T. rex?" (Cretaceous Carnivores)
- "Why?" (Lived in Cretaceous, ate meat)

**Minutes 50-60: Assessment & Wrap-Up**

Quiz (8 min):
- 10 questions, 1 point each
- Types: Matching (name to diet), Multiple choice (era identification)
- Example: "Which dino had a club tail?" "Name a Jurassic herbivore"

Scoring (2 min):
- Read answers aloud together
- Kids mark own or trade papers
- "Everyone who got 8+ is ready for next week!"

### WEEK 2: FOSSIL TYPES & FORMATION (60 minutes)

#### SETUP (Before Session)
- [ ] Gather: clay, plaster of paris, shells, water, containers
- [ ] Print fossil type sorting cards
- [ ] Print cross-section diagram
- [ ] Prepare sand/dirt + toy dinosaurs for burial demo
- [ ] Print 10-question quiz

#### SESSION TIMELINE

**Minutes 0-5: Engagement Hook**
- Show a real fossil (or good picture)
- "Is this the real dinosaur bone or a copy?"
- "How can you tell?"
- "Today, we're going to MAKE fossils!"

**Minutes 5-10: Intro to Fossils**
- "Fossils are preserved remains of ancient life"
- "There are 5 types, we're learning them today"
- "By the end, you'll be able to tell them apart"

**Minutes 10-25: Teaching the 5 Types**

Use BMCTC method:
- **B = Body Fossil:** Show picture of real bone in rock
  - "This is the ACTUAL bone"
  - "It turned to stone but it's the real thing"

- **M = Mold:** Show picture of empty snail shell impression
  - "The snail dissolved, left a hole"
  - "The hole is the MOLD"

- **C = Cast:** Show picture of plaster fossil
  - "Minerals filled the hole and hardened"
  - "Now it's a SOLID COPY"

- **T = Trace Fossil:** Show picture of footprint
  - "NO body part here!"
  - "Just the TRACK of the dinosaur"
  - "Behavior evidence!"

- **C = Carbonized Film:** Show picture of leaf outline
  - "Just a THIN BLACK LINE"
  - "Like a ghost picture"

**Minutes 25-45: Hands-On Demos (TWO EXPERIMENTS)**

**DEMO 1: Make a Mold & Cast (15 min)**

Materials:
- Modeling clay (1 lb)
- Plaster of paris
- Water
- Small container
- Shells or toys

Steps:
1. Press shell firmly into clay (2 min)
2. Remove shell â†’ Show the mold (hollow) (2 min)
3. Mix plaster per package directions (3 min)
4. Pour plaster into mold carefully (2 min)
5. Let set (while doing Demo 2)
6. Pop out cast â†’ Show solid replica (1 min)

Discussion:
- "In nature, what happens?" (Shell dissolves)
- "What's left?" (Empty space = MOLD)
- "What fills it?" (Minerals from water)
- "What do we get?" (Solid CAST = replica)

**DEMO 2: Rapid Burial Simulation (10 min)**

Materials:
- Clear jar
- Sand
- Water
- Toy dinosaur

Steps:
1. Layer sand at bottom (1 min)
2. Place toy dino on sand (1 min)
3. Quickly cover with more sand (rapid burial!) (1 min)
4. Add water carefully (2 min)
5. Observe: sand becomes cement-like (5 min)

Discussion:
- "Without rapid burial, what eats bones?" (Scavengers, bacteria)
- "What does burial do?" (Protects bones)
- "Why is water important?" (Brings minerals)
- "How long does this really take?" (Millions of years!)

**Minutes 45-55: Classification Activity**

Print 10 fossil descriptions on cards:
1. "A real dinosaur tooth in gray rock" â†’ BODY FOSSIL
2. "Hollow snail shape in limestone" â†’ MOLD
3. "Solid plaster copy of a trilobite" â†’ CAST
4. "Dinosaur footprint in sandstone" â†’ TRACE FOSSIL
5. "Thin black leaf outline on shale" â†’ CARBONIZED
6. "Real bones cemented together in rock" â†’ BODY FOSSIL
7. "Empty shell-shaped hole" â†’ MOLD
8. "Dinosaur burrow preserved in stone" â†’ TRACE FOSSIL
9. "Dino poop preserved in rock" â†’ TRACE FOSSIL
10. "Filled mold with shell shape" â†’ CAST

Activity (8 min):
- Read each description
- Students point/call out which type
- Discuss: "Why is this a trace fossil?" (No body, just behavior)
- Quick check for understanding

**Minutes 55-60: Assessment**

Quick Quiz (5 min):
- "What's a trace fossil?" (Answer: Behavior evidence, no body)
- "What's the difference between mold and cast?" (Mold = empty, cast = filled)
- "Which fossil type is the real bone?" (Body fossil)
- Draw simple fossil cross-section
- Label: mold, cast, sediment layer

### WEEK 3: GEOLOGIC TIME & EXTINCTION (60 minutes)

#### SETUP (Before Session)
- [ ] Get 6-meter roll of paper (or tape paper strips together)
- [ ] Print dinosaur cards from Week 1
- [ ] Print extinction evidence cards
- [ ] Print 10-question quiz
- [ ] Have markers/colored pencils

#### SESSION TIMELINE

**Minutes 0-5: Hook**
- Show asteroid impact video (30 seconds)
- "This is what happened 66 million years ago"
- "Today we learn why dinosaurs went extinct"

**Minutes 5-15: Teaching (3 Key Concepts)**

1. **The Three Eras** (5 min)
   - Draw timeline with three sections
   - Triassic: Hot, dry, small dinos, Pangaea together
   - Jurassic: Warm, humid, HUGE dinos, continents splitting
   - Cretaceous: Tropical, variety of dinos, then BOOM

2. **Extinction Evidence** (5 min)
   - Iridium layer: "This metal is in space but not on Earth normally"
   - Chicxulub crater: "6-mile-wide asteroid hit Mexico"
   - Volcanic ash: "India had massive eruptions"
   - Discussion: "What causes extinction?" (Multiple factors)

3. **Why Birds Survived** (5 min)
   - "Modern birds ARE dinosaurs!"
   - Small size, feathers, fast metabolism, adaptable
   - "Chicken = living dinosaur"

**Minutes 15-40: Main Activity - BUILD A TIMELINE**

Setup (5 min):
- Unroll 6-meter paper
- Mark 3 sections: Triassic (0-50cm), Jurassic (50-115cm), Cretaceous (115-180cm)
- Label each section
- Add climate notes

Activity (20 min):
- Students place dinosaur cards on timeline
- Add drawn pictures (quick sketches)
- Label key facts
- Walk the timeline together
- "Coelophysis lived here... Brachiosaurus here... T. rex here!"

**Minutes 40-55: Evidence Sorting Activity**

Print/laminate 8 evidence cards:
1. Iridium layer
2. Shocked quartz
3. Chicxulub crater (6 miles wide)
4. Deccan Traps lava (India)
5. Global darkness
6. Temperature drop
7. Mass starvation
8. Ash layer worldwide

Activity (12 min):
- Show each card
- Students sort into 3 piles:
  * **Asteroid Evidence**
  * **Volcano Evidence**
  * **Effects on Life**
- Discuss: "Iridium proves what?" (Asteroid came from space)
- Discuss: "Ash proves what?" (Volcanism happened)

**Minutes 55-60: Assessment**

Quiz (5 min):
1. "Which era had the biggest dinosaurs?" (Jurassic)
2. "Which era did T. rex live in?" (Cretaceous)
3. "What caused extinction 66 million years ago?" (Asteroid + volcanoes)
4. "Which dinosaurs survived extinction?" (Birds)
5. "What is iridium and why is it important?" (Rare metal from space, proves asteroid)

### WEEK 4: PALEONTOLOGY TOOLS & SITES (60 minutes)

#### SETUP (Before Session)
- [ ] Prepare sandbox (fill with sand, bury 15 fossil replicas)
- [ ] Mark 4Ã—4 grid with string
- [ ] Gather: brushes, trowels, sieve, measuring tape, magnifying glass
- [ ] Print famous sites cards
- [ ] Print excavation notebook template

#### SESSION TIMELINE

**Minutes 0-5: Hook**
- Show paleontologist photo digging T. rex
- "How do scientists find and dig up dinosaurs?"
- "Today YOU become paleontologists!"

**Minutes 5-15: Teaching**

1. **Paleontology Tools** (5 min)
   - Show each tool
   - Demonstrate on sample:
     - Brush: Gentle movements
     - Hammer: Tap-tap-tap, not smash
     - Sieve: Shake to sort
     - Measuring tape: Measure depth
   - "Why gentle?" (Fossils are fragile!)

2. **The Dig Process** (5 min)
   - Survey: Look for fossils
   - Excavate: Dig carefully in layers
   - Document: Photo and measure everything
   - Extract: Free the fossil
   - Analyze: Clean and identify

3. **Famous Sites** (5 min)
   - Hell Creek (Montana): T. rex, Triceratops
   - Morrison (Colorado): Brachiosaurus, Diplodocus, Allosaurus
   - Yixian (China): Feathered dinosaurs
   - Gobi Desert (Mongolia): Velociraptor, eggs
   - North Carolina: Acrocanthosaurus!

**Minutes 15-50: MOCK FOSSIL DIG**

Setup (5 min):
- Explain the dig site
- Show grid system (numbered squares)
- Explain rules:
  * Use only brush and trowel (no hands!)
  * Try to find 3 items
  * Record each find:
    - What was it?
    - Which grid square?
    - How deep?
  * Be careful not to damage

Activity (30 min):
- Divide into 2-3 teams
- Each team takes a grid square (5-7 min)
- Teams find fossils, record in notebook
- Rotate to new grid square
- Continue until all teams dig

Debrief (5 min):
- Show all finds
- Discuss: "Which grid square had most fossils?"
- "What was deepest?" (Should be oldest)
- "What species might these be?" (ID practice)

**Minutes 50-60: Site Matching Activity & Assessment**

Activity (5 min):
- Show fossil pictures
- Match to correct site:
  * T. rex, Triceratops â†’ Hell Creek
  * Brachiosaurus, Allosaurus â†’ Morrison
  * Feathered dinos â†’ Yixian
  * Velociraptor â†’ Gobi
  * Acrocanthosaurus â†’ North Carolina!

Quiz (5 min):
- What tool do you use to gently remove sediment? (Brush)
- Why do you measure depth? (Older = deeper)
- Which site is in North Carolina? (ACT site - Acrocanthosaurus)
- What does "excavate" mean? (Dig carefully)
