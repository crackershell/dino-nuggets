Event Basics

What is Dino Nuggets?

In this event, you will show what you know about dinosaurs, fossils, and how fossils are made. You will work with a partner to answer questions and look at pictures or real fossils!

Key Details

  • Team Size: Up to 2 students.
  • Time: 60 minutes.
  • What to bring: Pencils. You can also bring up to 2 field guides or binders, and magnifying glasses.

Source: 2026 Division A Event Manual, Dino Nuggets section.

You need to know about these amazing creatures! You should know their names, what they ate (meat, plants, or both), and when they lived (Triassic, Jurassic, or Cretaceous).

3.2 Fossils & Fossilization

What is a fossil?

A fossil is the remains or traces of ancient life. It could be a bone, a shell, or even a footprint!

How do fossils form?

There are many ways fossils can form. Here are some you should know:

  • Petrification: Turning into stone.
  • Amber: Getting stuck in sticky tree resin.
  • Cast & Mold: Leaving a shape in the rock.
  • Trace Fossils: Things left behind like footprints or poop (coprolites)!

Study Plan

Here is a 4-week plan to help you get ready!

Week 1: The Basics
  • Read the "Dino Nuggets" rules with an adult.
  • Learn what a fossil is.
  • Look at pictures of the dinosaurs on the list.
Week 2: Fossil Types
  • Learn the different ways fossils form (like amber or molds).
  • Practice identifying Invertebrates (animals without backbones).
Week 3: Dino Details
  • Learn which dinosaurs were Carnivores (meat eaters) or Herbivores (plant eaters).
  • Learn about the Geologic Time Scale (Triassic, Jurassic, Cretaceous).
Week 4: Practice!
  • Take the practice quiz below.
  • Review your binder or field guide.
  • Practice working with your partner.
